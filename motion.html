Prerequisite Knowledge

1) Scalars and vectors

 On the basis of direction dependance physical quantities are divided into two categories 1)Scalar Quantities 2) Vector Quantities

Scalar Quantities:

Those physical quantities which representation does not require any knowledge of direction, are called scalar quantities. Such quantities require only magnitude(numerical values). Scalar quantities obey simple algebra for mathematical operations. Example: Time, mass, distance, speed, volume etc.

Vector Quantities:

Those physical quantities which representation requires magnitude and direction both, are called vector quantities. Such quantities must provide a knowledge of magnitude and the direction in which it is effective. Vector quantities do not obey simple algeraic rules. Vector algebra is used for their mathematical calculations. Vector quantities are expressed symbolically by putting an arrow over the alphabete and graphically by arrows. Illustration is given in the video. Example: Displacement, Velocity, acceleration, force etc.

2) System of measurement:

To measure a quantity we first choose a standard of same kind and then compare the quantity with the chosen standard. Hence measurement is just a comparision of a quantity with the chosen standard of same kind. The chosen standard is called unit. Example: If we want to measure distance between two points then first of all we have to take anything as a standard which has length. If the distance is n times between the two points then Q=nu. Where Q is the quantity, n is the numerical value and u is the unit.
